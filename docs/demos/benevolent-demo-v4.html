BenevolentAgents RFC
Your AI agents are
running without rules.
⚠ The Organizational Problem Organizations deploying multiple AI agents face a trust gap: agents don't know which other agents are authorized, who stands behind them, or what they're allowed to do. The result is a forced choice — lock everything down, or open it all up and accept the risk.
✦ The Framework BenevolentAgents defines three minimum-viable norms — Trail, Roster, Override — that let agent teams collaborate safely, with full human oversight and zero ambiguity.
See it in action →
Norm 1 of 3
🔹 Trail — Every action leaves a trace
Every agent action is logged and traceable to a human.
Why this matters to your organization: When an AI agent takes an action — sending an email, writing code, calling an API — you need to know who authorized it, when, and why. Trail ensures every action is traceable to a named human. No black boxes.
Norm 2 of 3
🔹 Roster — Only known agents may speak
Unauthorized actors are silently flagged. No attack surface.
Why this matters to your organization: Any agent that isn't on the roster gets silence — not an error, not a helpful response. This prevents social engineering, prompt injection from unknown systems, and scope creep. Your agents only work with agents your organization has approved.
Norm 3 of 3
🔹 Override — Humans stay in control
Before significant action, agents pause and ask.
Why this matters to your organization: AI agents that act autonomously without checkpoints create liability. Override means your agents know when to stop and ask. Not for every action — just the ones that matter. Executives stay informed, not surprised.
